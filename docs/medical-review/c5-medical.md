# Clinical Review — c5 Medical Emergencies

**Sections reviewed:** 11 (5.1–5.11) · **Items:** ~31 quiz + ~84 flashcards
**Overall:** 🟢 **Clinically excellent — no errors.** One terminology item to verify.

## 🟢 Confirmed sound (highlights)
- Respiratory: silent chest = failure; PE after long flight; COPD over-oxygenation drive — correct.
- Neuro: Cincinnati scale, status epilepticus (>5 min), hypoglycemia mimicking stroke — correct.
- Endocrine: DKA (Kussmaul/fruity) vs **HHS** (glucose 940, no ketosis) — correctly distinguished.
- **Anaphylaxis:** epi 0.3 mg IM anterolateral thigh; 2-organ-system rule; repeat dosing — correct.
- Tox: opioid → **airway/BVM before naloxone**; **CO poisoning with falsely-normal SpO₂**; **TCA
  (amitriptyline) Na-channel blockade → wide QRS/seizures** — all correct and high-quality.
- GI: appendicitis migration, AAA (no deep palpation), upper GI bleed + orthostasis — correct.
- Renal: atypical UTI in elderly; **hyperkalemia (peaked T waves) from missed dialysis** — correct.
- **Sepsis (5.9): qSOFA = 2 (AMS + RR ≥22 + SBP ≤100)** and septic shock = refractory hypotension —
  correct.
- Behavioral: panic-attack "rule out medical first"; **prone-restraint positional asphyxia →
  reposition lateral** — correct.
- Pain: Wong-Baker FACES for a child; nitrous for labor pain — correct.

## 🟠 Verify
1. **5.10 Q1/Q3 — "excited delirium."** The clinical management (cooling, de-escalation, avoid prone
   restraint, monitor) is correct, but the **term "excited delirium" is contested** and has been
   formally rejected by several bodies (e.g., AMA 2023). Consider relabeling as **"hyperactive
   delirium with severe agitation"** while keeping the (correct) management.
