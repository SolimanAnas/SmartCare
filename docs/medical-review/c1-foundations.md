# Clinical Review — c1 Foundations of Care

**Sections reviewed:** 11 (1.1–1.11) · **Items:** ~132 quiz + ~88 flashcards
**Overall:** Strong. Legal/ethical, consent/refusal, terminology, and documentation content is
accurate and well-constructed. One clear error and two items to verify.

## 🔴 Errors (fix before release)

1. **1.8 Documentation — Q11: "What does the 'P' in CHART narrative format stand for?" → "Patient history"**
   The **CHART** mnemonic has **no "P"**: it is **C**hief complaint, **H**istory, **A**ssessment,
   **R**x (treatment), **T**ransport. The question is invalid as written.
   *Fix:* change the letter to **H** ("History") — or replace with a valid CHART element (e.g.,
   "What does the 'R' in CHART stand for?" → "Rx / treatment given").

## 🟠 Verify (source/protocol-dependent)

2. **1.4 Infection & PPE — Q12: meningococcal meningitis → "add eye protection **and a gown** — droplet precautions"**
   Meningococcal disease requires **droplet precautions = surgical mask + eye protection** (plus
   routine gloves). A **gown** is **contact-precaution** PPE and is not required by droplet
   precautions. The answer conflates the two.
   *Fix:* the correct "additional PPE" is **eye protection** (mask already worn); drop the gown,
   or change the rationale.

## 🟡 Nuance (optional clarification)

3. **1.4 — Q4: HIV PEP "ideally initiated within 2 hours."** Reasonable as an *ideal* target, but
   the standard teaching is **as soon as possible** (every hour counts), effective up to **72 h**.
   Consider wording "as soon as possible, ideally within 1–2 hours (up to 72 h)."

4. **1.3 — "leading cause of on-duty EMS death = struck by a vehicle."** Defensible, but many
   sources cite **transportation/ambulance collisions** as the single leading cause. Confirm against
   your source; both road-related, but the distinction matters for a test item.
