# Clinical Review — c4 Cardiovascular & Resuscitation

**Sections reviewed:** 13 (4.1–4.13, incl. new 4.12 Pediatric Arrest & 4.13 TOR) · **Items:** ~38 quiz + ~98 flashcards
**Overall:** 🟢 **Clinically accurate and unusually sophisticated.** No medical errors. Two notes below.

## 🟢 Confirmed sound (highlights)
- Anatomy/conduction: mitral LA↔LV, SA node 60–100, S1 = AV-valve closure — correct.
- **ACS:** STEMI = complete occlusion/transmural necrosis; aspirin 160–325 mg chewed; **inferior MI
  + nitro → hypotension = RV infarction (preload-dependent)** — excellent, correct.
- Hypertensive: emergency = end-organ damage; **aortic dissection** (ripping pain to back, inter-arm
  BP differential); **Cushing's triad = ↑ICP** — correct.
- Dysrhythmia/ECG: sinus brady (atropine 0.5 mg/pacing), atrial flutter sawtooth, complete heart
  block + escape → pacing; PR 4 small boxes = 0.16 s; **inferior STEMI → V4R, avoid nitro** — correct.
- Arrest/AED: low EtCO₂ → improve compressions; post-shock → resume compressions immediately;
  pediatric arrest with adult pads → AP placement; hairy chest → shave & reapply — correct.
- Post-ROSC: EtCO₂ rise = earliest ROSC sign; **wean O₂ to 94–98% (avoid hyperoxia)**; STEMI → PCI — correct.
- Shock/fluids: Class III hemorrhage features; LR contraindicated in liver disease; **cautious 250 mL
  challenge in cardiogenic shock with pulmonary edema**; 70 kg × 20 mL/kg = 1400 mL — correct.
- New 4.12 (pediatric: respiratory cause, 15:2 two-rescuer, CPR if HR<60 + poor perfusion) and
  4.13 (rigor/lividity, medical direction, honor DNR) — correct.

## 🟡 Nuance / verify
1. **4.6 Q3 — "new LBBB + ischemic symptoms = STEMI equivalent → activate cath lab."** This is the
   classic teaching, but current guidelines refine it: a new LBBB is **not automatically** a STEMI;
   **Sgarbossa criteria** are used to identify true occlusion. Acceptable at EMT level, but consider
   softening ("a STEMI *equivalent that warrants urgent evaluation*").

2. **Scope note (not an error):** Much of c4 is **ALS-level** content — atropine dosing,
   transcutaneous pacing, right-sided leads, IV fluid boluses, vasopressors. Clinically correct, but
   **beyond EMT-Basic scope**. Given the "prehospital EMT" framing, decide whether to (a) keep as
   enrichment/"good to know," (b) tag these as AEMT/Paramedic-level, or (c) trim to EMT scope.
