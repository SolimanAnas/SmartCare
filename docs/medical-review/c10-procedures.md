## 🟢 Confirmed sound (highlights)
- Airway skills: OPA sizing (corner of mouth–earlobe); trauma airway = jaw-thrust → suction → NPA
  (assess for basilar skull fracture); over-ventilation complications — correct.
- Bleeding control: tighten tourniquet until **all** bleeding stops (venous-only occlusion worsens
  bleeding); **junctional wound → hemostatic packing**; tourniquet placement; **do not release a
  hemorrhage-controlling tourniquet in the field** — correct.
- Splinting: immobilize joint above & below; **traction splint contraindicated with pelvic fracture**;
  shoulder dislocation = sling/swath (no reduction) — correct.
- SMR: NEXUS-based clearance, mechanism-alone does not mandate SMR, minimize backboard time,
  backboard → respiratory compromise management — correct.
- Movement: power-lift principles, stair-chair feet-first, helicopter LZ wind change, bariatric
  Reeves sleeve — correct.
- **12-lead ECG:** V1 = 4th ICS right of sternum; **inferior STEMI + anterior ST depression → V4R +
  posterior V7–V9**; **ECG within 10 min** of contact — correct.
- Med admin: NTG/PDE-5 hold; no oral glucose if can't swallow → glucagon; **pediatric anaphylaxis epi
  0.15 mg IM (<25 kg) vastus lateralis** — correct.

## 🟡 Nuance
1. **10.2 Q1 — E-cylinder duration "≈30 min" at 1,600 psi / 15 L/min.** Using the standard E-factor
   (0.28) **with a 200 psi safe residual**: (1600−200)×0.28 ÷ 15 ≈ **26 min**. The "30 min" answer
   omits the safe-residual subtraction. Minor — confirm which convention you teach.
2. **10.3 Q4 — rationale for not releasing a tourniquet lists "air embolism."** The real concerns are
   **reperfusion/metabolic washout and rebleeding**; "air embolism" isn't a typical tourniquet-release
   complication. The *answer* (do not release) is correct — just tidy the rationale.
