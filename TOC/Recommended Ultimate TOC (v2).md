## What changed from v1 (and why)

| v1 | v2 | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 15 sections mixing content + features | App features **at top** (kept prominent), curriculum = 10 study chapters | Users open the app for Courses/Exams/Tools — keep those one tap away; keep the *study* tree clean |
| No exam mapping | Every chapter tagged to its **NREMT domain** | Enables "study by weak exam area" + course alignment |
| Cardiac arrest buried under "Cardiovascular & Shock" | `c4` renamed **Cardiovascular & Resuscitation** (CPR/AED/ROSC explicit) | NREMT weights resuscitation heavily |
| Nurse scope implicit | `[RN]` topics woven into domains (IV, ESI, EHR, sedation, suturing assist) | One role-tagged curriculum for EMT **and** ER nurses |
| `c4` Shock split from cardio | Shock kept in `c4` (cardio) + trauma shock in `c6` | Matches how shock is taught (perfusion failure) without a thin standalone chapter |

## Open options to consider
1. **Anatomy & Physiology** — currently woven into `c2`. If you want a dedicated A&P chapter, split it into its own `c11` (some users like a standalone reference).
2. **Resuscitation as its own chapter** — alternative to folding into `c4`, if you want BLS/ACLS-style prominence.
3. **Scope tags in UI** — render the `[RN]` tag as a small badge so EMTs can hide nurse-only topics (filter by role).
